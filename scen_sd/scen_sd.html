Use Case 1: Passenger Makes A Hall Call

Scenario 1A: Passenger arrives at a hallway while elevator doors are closed.

Author name: David Thompson // davidtho

Pre-Conditions:

  • All doors are closed.
  • Passenger has already arrived at a hallway to use the elevator.
  • Dispatcher has desired floor and direction.

Scenario:

  1. Passenger pushes hall call (up or down) button for passenger's desired direction d.
  2. Passenger sees that corresponding hall button light turns on.
  3. Passenger releases hall call button
  4. Dispatcher recomputes desired floor

Post-Conditions:

  • All doors are closed.
  • Hall button light for passenger's desired direction is on.
  • Dispatcher has desired floor and direction.

Sequence Diagram:

Scenario 1B: Passenger arrives at a hallway when elevator is already there and the car is traveling in the same direction as desired by passenger, but the doors have begun closing for the first time.

Author name: David Thompson // davidtho

Pre-Conditions:

  • Car is at same floor as passenger.
  • Car is traveling in same direction d as desired by passenger.
  • At least one door is open.
  • HallLight[f,b,d] for passenger's desired direction is off.

Scenario:

  1. Doors at hallway b starts closing for the first time.
  2. Passenger arrives at floor f hallway b to use the elevator, with intent to travel in direction d.
  3. Passenger presses hall call button before doors are fully closed.
  4. HallLight[f,b,d] turns on.
  5. Passenger releases hall call button.
  6. Dispatcher recomputes desired floor f, hallway b.
  7. Passenger observes HallLight[f,b,d] turn off.
  8. Doors are commanded to open.
  9. CountDown is set to Dwell

Post-Conditions:

  • Elevator is at the passenger's floor.
  • Doors at hallway b are opening.
  • Hall button light for passenger's desired direction is off.

Sequence Diagram:

Scenario 1C: Passenger arrives at a hallway when elevator is already there and the car is traveling in the same direction as desired by passenger, but the doors have begun closing for the second time.

Author name: David Thompson // davidtho

Pre-Conditions:

  • Car is at same floor f as passenger.
  • Car is traveling in the same direction as direction d desired by passenger.
  • At least one door is open.
  • Doors have already been commanded to close once on this floor.
  • Hall button light for passenger's desired direction is off.
  • Dispatcher has desired floor and direction.
  • Door at hallway b has begun closing for the second time.

Scenario:

  1. Passenger arrives at floor f hallway b to use the elevator going in direction d.
  2. Passenger presses hall call button before doors are fully closed.
  3. Hall button light turns on.
  4. Passenger releases hall call button.
  5. Dispatcher recomputes desired floor and direction.

Post-Conditions:

  • Elevator is at the passenger's floor.
  • Doors are closing.
  • Hall button light for passenger's desired direction is on.
  • Dispatcher has desired floor and direction.

Use Case 2: Passenger Makes A Car Call

Scenario 2A: Passenger is in the car and elevator is not at the desired destination floor.

Author name: David Thompson // davidtho

Pre-Conditions:

  • Passenger is in the car and wants to go to destination floor f and hallway b; Elevator's desiredFloor != {f,b,*}.
  • Car call button for the passenger's desired destination is not lit.
  • mDriveSpeed was last received as {s,d}
  • mDoorCommand for all doors was last received as Stop

Scenario:

  1. Passenger presses CarCall[f,b] for their destination.
  2. Passenger observes that the button for their destination has lit up.
  3. Passenger releases car call button
  4. Dispatcher computes the desired floor.

Post-Conditions:

  • Passenger is in the car and wants to go to destination floor f and hallway b; Elevator's desiredFloor has been recomputed.
  • Car call button for the passenger's desired destination is lit.
  • mDriveSpeed was last received as {s,d}
  • mDoorCommand for all doors was last received as Stop

Sequence Diagram:

Scenario 2B: Passenger is in the car and the elevator has arrived at the passenger's desired floor, but passenger has not yet exited the car as the doors begin to close for the first time.

Author name: David Thompson // davidtho

Pre-Conditions:

  • Passenger is in the car.
  • Elevator has arrived at the desired floor f and hallway b, but the passenger has not yet exited the car.
  • At least one door is open.
  • The car light for the passenger's floor is off.

Scenario:

  1. Doors start to close.
  2. Passenger presses car call button before doors are fully closed.
  3. Passenger observes car light turn on.
  4. Passenger releases car call button.
  5. Dispatcher recomputes desired floor f and hallway b.
  6. Passenger observes car light turn off.
  7. Doors are commanded to open.
  8. CountDown is set to Dwell

Post-Conditions:

  • The passenger is still in the elevator at his destination floor.
  • The doors are opening.
  • The car light for the passenger's floor is off.

    Use Case 3: Elevator Stops at Floor f with DesiredDirection d

    Scenario 3A: Elevator stops at floor f, CarLight[f,b] and HallLight[f,b,d] turn off

    Author name: Daniel Santoro // ddsantor

    Pre-Conditions:

    • Car is moving towards floor f and hallway b.
    • The mAtFloor[f,b] was last received as False.
    • CarLight[f,b] is on.
    • HallLight[f,b,d] is on.
    • Doors at hallway b are closed.
    • Dispatcher has sent mDesiredFloor[f, b, d].
    • Car has arrived at floor

    Scenario:

    1. Countdown for doors is set to Dwell.
    2. Doors at hallway b are commanded to open.
    3. CarLight[f,b] turns off.
    4. HallLight[f,b,d] turns off.
    5. Doors at hallway b finish opening.
    6. Countdown decrements to zero.
    7. Doors at hallway b are commanded to Close

    Post-Conditions:

    • Car at floor f and hallway b.
    • CarLight[f,b] is off.
    • HallLight[f,b,d] is off.
    • Doors at hallway b are Closing.

    Sequence Diagram:

    Use Case 6: Passenger Triggers Door Reversal

    Scenario 6A: Passenger obstructs the doorway for the first time while doors are closing

    Author name: David Thompson // davidtho

    Pre-Conditions:

    • Passenger is at floor f.
    • Car is stopped at floor f and hallway b.
    • At least one door[b,r] is open.
    • There has not yet been a door reversal on this floor for this stop.

    Scenario:

    1. Door[b,r] begins closing.
    2. Passenger moves into Door [b,r]'s threshold and triggers its reversal sensor
    3. Doors[b,*] are commanded to open.
    4. CountDown is set to Dwell
    5. Doors[b,*] complete opening.
    6. CountDown decrements to zero
    7. Doors[b,*] begin to close

    Post-Conditions:

    • Elevator is at floor f.
    • Doors [b,*] are closing.

    Sequence Diagram:

    Scenario 6B: Passenger obstructs the doorway for the second time while doors are closing

    Author name: David Thompson // davidtho

    Pre-Conditions:

    • Passenger is at floor f.
    • Car is stopped at floor f and hallway b.
    • At least one door[b,r] is open.
    • Dispatcher has desired floor g

    Scenario:

    1. Doors[b,r] begin closing.
    2. Passenger moves into Door b's threshold and triggers its reversal sensor
    3. Doors[b,*] are commanded to nudge.
    4. Dispatcher has desired floor [f, b, d]
    5. Doors continue to nudge.
    6. Doors finish closing.

    Post-Conditions:

    • Elevator is at floor f.
    • Doors [b,*] are nudging.

    Use Case 7: Passenger Reads Car Position

    Scenario 7A: Passenger is in the car, which is moving from floor f towards floor g (the next floor in the direction of travel)

    Author name: David Thompson // davidtho

    Pre-Conditions:

    • Passenger last read floor f on the Car Position Indicator.
    • All elevator doors are closed.
    • Car is moving in the direction d equal to the direction from floor f towards floor g.
    • mDriveSpeed was last received as [s, d].

    Scenario:

    1. Car Position Controller receives Drive Speed message mDriveSpeed = [s', d].
    2. Car Position Controller receives Car Level Position message.
    3. Passenger observes car position indicator change from displaying floor f to floor g.
    4. Car Position Controller receives Car Level Position message for slow speed.
    5. Car arrives at floor g moving at slow speed
    6. Passenger observes car position indicator value of g

    Post-Conditions:

    • Passenger last read floor g on the Car Position Indicator.
    • All elevator doors are closed.
    • Car is moving in the direction d equal to the direction from floor f towards floor g.

    Use Case 9: Drive Moves Car to Next Floor

    Scenario 9A: Car is moving from floor f to floor g and the Doors open..

    Author name: Daniel Santoro // ddsantor

    Pre-Conditions:

    • Elevator is moving in the hoistway.

    Scenario:

    1. Doors are read as open.
    2. Dispatcher sets mDesiredFloor to {1, NONE, STOP}

    Post-Conditions:

    • Dispatcher has set mDesiredFloor to {1, NONE, STOP}

    Sequence Diagram:

    Scenario 9B: Drive moves the elevator from {floor f, hallway b} to {floor g, hallway c} in direction d using fast speed.

    Author name: Daniel Santoro // ddsantor

    Pre-Conditions:

    • Elevator is at floor f, with at least one Door[b,r] open.
    • Car is level.
    • Dispatcher has computed desired floor g and hallway c.
    • Drive is stopped.

    Scenario:

    1. All doors close.
    2. Drive is commanded accelerate car in direction d at speed Slow, then to Fast.
    3. Drive reads CarLevelPosition and DriveSpeed to calculate commit point.
    4. Drive moves car in direction d at speed Slow.
    5. Car arrives at desired floor g.
    6. Drive is commanded to stop.

    Post-Conditions:

    • Car at desired floor g.
    • All doors are closed.

    Sequence Diagram:

    • Elevator is in the hoistway moving from f to g and has received a new DesiredFloor = {h, e, d}.

    Scenario:

    1. Drive reads CarLevelPosition and DriveSpeed to calculate commit point.
    2. Drive moves car in direction d at speed Slow.
    3. Car arrives at desired floor h.
    4. Drive is commanded to stop.

    Post-Conditions:

    • Car at desired floor g.
